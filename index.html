Introduction

The case study and it's reseach

Living with a chronic disease can be challenging. The Health companion app aims to alleviate some of the strain of managing medicines and doctor's appointments for patients with chronic illnesses. This case study describes the UX/UI design approach for creating such an app. To understand what our target audience requires, we must first identify who they are. The research section of this case study focused on determining who the target audience is and what impairments or obstacles individuals with chronic diseases would face when using the health companion app.

  • See the Research

Personas and their journey

The research findings led to the creation of three personas: Eleanor, who lives with dementia and osteoporosis. Denise, her daughter and primary caretaker, and Thomas, a married man who lives with diabetes. All three persona will be taken along a user journey, sometimes using only a subset of the functionalities of the app, to discover their potential reaction and feelings towards the Health companion app.

Two modes

The Health companion app, as I envisioned it, may be configured in two modes: Self-Care mode, which provides users with a more comprehensive interface and simple access to modification menus. And the Caretaker mode, which provides the user with a simpler interface after the app has been configured by the caretaker to eliminate distractions and limit confusion.

Notes on features

The app features medication reminders, logs that can be sent as reports to the helath professionals,Health professionals appointment schedueling.The app also offers a feature that calls emergency services and displays a list of medications the user has taken within the last day on the screen. There were also intentions to include reminders to monitor blood pressure and blood sugar to the reminders. A mood tracker option has also been included, and the data can be saved and reported just like the medicine intake log. The motivation for adding this feature derives from the recognition that people living with chronic diseases are more likely to experience depression and mood disorders, and blood sugar levels can also influence mood.

Usability Goals and Test

In addition to medication reminders and medical appointments, the app offers a feature that calls emergency services and displays a list of medications that the user has taken on the screen. There were also intentions to include reminders to monitor blood pressure and blood sugar to the reminders.

Effectiveneess

  • Caregivers are often very busy people and have a lot to take care of. People with chronic diseases might be tired or not have a lot of time to put to setup the app. Setting up the app for a loved one should not take more than 8 minuutes once the list of pills is acquired through the medical professional. If it takes too much time to set up the app, the users might never do it and stay with their current routine ,even if it means possibly skipping doses.
  • Adding a doctors appointment should take no more than 6 clicks.
  • Adding a pill should take no more than 4 clicks.
  • Calling emergency services should be a 1 click task.
  • Snoozing or ackowledging that you have taken your pill should be a 1 click task.

Safety

This is very important as skipping a dose or taking too much medication can lead to serious health problems.
  • The buttons for "dose taken" and "snooze" should be very clear from one another.
  • In the last dose taken , users can modify the time they have taken the dose if a mistake is made.
  • The emergency call can be cancelled.

Utility

App Modes

  • he app will have two modes: Simple and "Self care mode" to differentiate the number of tabs available
  • The app has an Emergency tab that calls the Emergency and shows the list of medications on the screen.
  • The app offers the option to add a daily mood tracker where the user can click on an icon to log their mood.

Medication and monitoring reminders

  • Users must have a tab where they can update their medication list, set a schedule which can include a time-between-pills constraint. The user can also set a discontinue date for the alert, for temporary pills such as antibiotics.
  • The app should open a foreground alert page when it's time to take a pill.
  • In simple mode, the app should send an email to the user or the caregiver when a pill is due for a refill.
  • In caregiver mode, the app sends a notification to the user, and the update medication tab has a button to call the pharmacy to ask for a refill.
  • The app should let the user modify the time they took the pill.
  • The app allows reminders to take blood sugar and blood pressure readings and keeps a log of them.

Doctor Information and Appointment

  • The app offers a Doctor's information tab where more than one specialist can be added with their information.
  • The app lets the user generate a report for the doctor with the pill list and the logs. The report highlights missed doses.
  • In the doctor's tab, there is a button to add a new appointment. The appointment is added to the user's Google Calendar.

Learnability

  • A single run through the app with the user is enough for the user to be able to start using the app.
  • Update menus are in the same format as the setup , the user does not have to learn the interface again

Memorability

  • The App buttons are intuitive and the goal of each tab is clear.
  • All the buttons that can be pressed are the same color.

Some Design decisions

Colors

Colorblind safe palette

The choice of color was made to be as inclusive as possible. Recommendations in the NCEAS Science Communication Resource Corner - colorblind Safe Color Schemeswere followed.

The color palette chosen was heavily inspired by thePaul to color blind safe palette BuRd variant.

The Health Companion color palette

Here is the color palette chosen for the Health Companion app.

The prototype was tested by somebody who is colorblind and they noted that the light blue and the grey could not be well distinguished. As a result, these two colors were only used in the background or for non-impactful details.

Consistency

Based on the usability goals, the app must be easy to use and not be confusing. For this reason, the same yellow color was used consistently for every clickable item in the app. This helps avoid confusion for people with cognitive problems as well as low vision.

A combination of color , text and icons was used to distinguish critical buttons from each other

Font

The font chosen for the app is the google font Inter. It is a sans-serif font that was designed for computer screens making this a good choice for a mobile application. The font size is never smaller than 16 px, but often larger when the frame permitted it. The font size was chosen to be large enough for people with low vision to read easily and to draw attention to important elements.

Reflection and Conclusion

The UX/UI design process of researching the target audience, creating personas based on the results, bringing the personas along on the health companion interaction journey map helped shape the focus of the UI/UX design. The design was decided to be more utilitarian than beautiful, with the goal of making the app helpful and accessible to those who need it while avoiding generating pain points for those users. CConducting user research and developing personas also enabled the development of clear usability goals. One of the challenge of the design process was combining the different needs while keeping the application simple. The hardware constraint was also an obstacle because mobile apps typically have smaller screens than desktop computers, and designing an interface for those who require a lot of space around clickable components and legible text required extra creativity. The whole thought process and creation was guided by the user needs and reality as well as known best practices for designing for those specifc needs instead of guessing and going with personal preferences. The UX/UI design process guided the decisions and allowed the creation of an interface that is functional and simple for the target audience to use.
